L14: Audio Telemetry

Governance decisions become sound. A healthy system sounds like a chord. An attack sounds like noise. You can hear security.

Challenge: Play "Healthy Fleet" then "Under Attack." Close your eyes — can you tell which is which? That's the point. Operators can monitor security with their ears while looking at other things.

How it maps:
- ALLOW = C major (523Hz sine) — clean, consonant
- QUARANTINE = E flat (311Hz triangle) — tense, questioning
- ESCALATE = G sharp (415Hz square) — sharp, urgent
- DENY = B flat (233Hz sawtooth) — harsh, grinding

A healthy fleet produces a pleasant melody (all ALLOW). As attacks increase, the melody degrades into dissonance. A full attack session sounds like noise — the audio entropy mirrors the security entropy.

Real-world use: SOC operators monitoring large fleets can have audio telemetry in the background. A sudden shift from consonance to dissonance draws attention without requiring eyes-on-screen.
