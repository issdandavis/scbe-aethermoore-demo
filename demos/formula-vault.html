How it works:
1. Your formula is hashed with SHA-256 (the same algorithm the USPTO uses)
2. The hash is encoded in Sacred Tongues (a 256-token conlang vocabulary — our proprietary layer)
3. Everything is signed with HMAC-SHA-256 and timestamped
4. You get a certificate proving THIS data existed at THIS time, signed by THIS person

What this proves: If someone later claims your formula, you have cryptographic evidence with a specific timestamp showing you had it first. The SHA-256 hash is verifiable by anyone. The Sacred Tongue encoding adds a second verification layer unique to SCBE.

Like WIPO PROOF (the World Intellectual Property Organization's timestamping service), but free for individuals, with Sacred Tongue encoding on top, and you can run it locally:
pip install scbe-aethermoore
